Welcome to MCA Model UN

Our school's premier club for politics and international relations. Model UN centers around students participating as delegates for different nations, collaborating with one another to craft resolutions that target global problems.

Our Story What is Model UN

Meetings

Held virtually once a week, these workshops are great for new delegates starting their journey into MUN, and for experienced delegates to learn more about the inner nuances. We start off by going over slides to help you with the various aspects of MUN, take questions, and finish with some fun activities! !

Conferences

Participating in conferences are a great way to work to become a better MUN delegate, improving your public speaking and ability to work with others. We participate in many regional conferences and 2-3 national conferences a year.

Global Awareness

By participating in MUN, you will gain a much broader understanding of the world outside of what we see in our daily lives. From lack of access to clean water, to human trafficking, and discussing destablization, war, and conflict in various parts of the world, Model UN makes us realize how fortunate we are and what we can do to help.

MUN is FUN

Through MUN, you will meet people from across the world! Delegates at conferences come from a variety of backgrounds, and many of you will form long-lasting friendships with the people in your committees. On top of that, the memories you make with your friends will last a lifetime! Any doubts? Check out the Gallery!

About

Find Out More About Us

Read the story about how MUN started and became one of the largest clubs at our school in just 3 years!

MUN emerged at our school in the 2021-2022 school year as an unofficial club formed by four Model UN delegates in middle school and high school: Shivam Syal, Reva Amritkar, Aashish Codada, and Sanjana Ravuri. That year, the first MCAMUNC was held in the Woodbridge Public Library, and a team was selected to go to ILMUNC XXXVIII.

From these humble beginnings, the club has grown considerably. In the 2022-2023 school year, MCAMUNC II was held, and we attend PMUNC and ILMUNC. This upcoming year, we look forward to MCAMUNC III, and plan on sending delegations to PMUNC, ILMUNC, and CMUNCE.

We also participate in smaller, regional conferences, such as SOUTHMUNC, NBTHSMUN, and JPSMUN, just to name a few. Unlike national conferences, anyone can participate in these, so they are great for new delegates!

Conferences

Check out our Conferences

At the core of Model UN is the conferences that delegates attend, working in their committees to create resolutions that tackle global issues, and hopefully win some awards along the way!

Princeton Model UN Conference (PMUNC)

Held from November 16th-19th, 2023 by Princeton University, this is one of the national conferences that we send a team for every year. In 2022, we were named as an Outstanding Small Delegation, and hope to do even better this year!

Ivy League Model UN Conference (ILMUNC)

Held from January 25th-28th, 2024 by the University Pennsylvania, this is another national conference which we participate in. Last year, we had over 10 delegates earn awards, including one gavel!

Columbia Model UN Conference (CMUNCE)

Held from January 11th-14th, 2024 by Columbia University, this is a national conference that we are considering participation in for the first time. Stay tuned for more information about the conference!

SOUTHMUNC

Held by WWP South, this is a smaller, regional MUN conference which we attended on March 25, 2023, and will be attending in 2024. Conferences like this are highly recommended for newer delegates, providing a great experience for those just entering the world of Model UN!

MCAMUNC III

MCAMUNC is the flagship, beginner-friendly, conference that we hold every year for students at our school. It provides an entry into Model UN for students (for most of the executive board it was their first ever conference), as they work to create, what is for the most them, their first resolution! This conference is also used to determine the team we send for our national conferences, and we had over 50 delegates participate last year!

EAGLEMUNC

Working with JP Stevens, this is a joint-conference that we hope to hold in the spring of 2024. Subscribe to be notified of any updates regarding this conference, registration, and commitees!
